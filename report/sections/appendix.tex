\subsection{Corrected Branching Evaluation}
\label{app:joao_branching_model_selection}

The final branching evaluation uses a leakage-free temporal case split and a
common BPMN-replay decision dataset. Model and feature selection are performed
only on the development partition, while the held-out partition is reserved
for final evaluation.

The $76$ cases used in the fixed-replay allocation benchmark are contained in
the held-out partition and have zero overlap with the evaluation-model training
cases. Historical full-log and pre-optimization models are retained only as
development evidence and are not used to support the final held-out claims.

The replay procedure separates process-model coverage from predictive
performance. Deterministic states are recorded but excluded from the
classification benchmark because they expose only one possible successor.
Observations whose historical next activity is not enabled after replay are
reported as BPMN-alignment limitations rather than ordinary prediction errors.

The diagnostics show that the predictive reservation mechanism was operational,
but only $474$ of the $1{,}580$ created reservations were eventually used.
This helps explain why ParkSong-Composite achieved full route completion but
did not improve cycle or waiting time relative to the reactive baselines.

The limited resource--activity processing-time coverage also requires fallback
estimates for combinations not represented in the training split. The ParkSong
results therefore demonstrate an executable anticipatory mechanism, while also
showing the cost of strategic idling under imperfect predictions and incomplete
processing-time coverage.
