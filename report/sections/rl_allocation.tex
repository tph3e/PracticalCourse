\subsection{Reinforcement-Learning Allocation Policy}\label{subsec:rl}
\textit{(Aldo Patrone)}\quad
The allocation policy is learned by modeling the problem as in Middelhuis et
al.~\cite{middelhuis2025rl}: a case-based discrete-event environment, \emph{calibrated
to the real log} (arrival rate and case length matched to BPIC-17), where at each
decision step the agent assigns a resource to a ready activity instance \emph{or
postpones}. The reward faithfully encodes a disclosed, equally-weighted objective of
\emph{cycle time and fairness}: a per-case inverse-cycle-time reward plus
potential-based shaping on the Gini of resource loads, whose episode sum telescopes
to the negative final Gini, i.e.\ exactly the fairness metric of
Sec.~\ref{ssub:metrics}. The variable action space is handled two ways: a from-scratch
per-candidate-scoring policy (compact MLP, REINFORCE, pure \emph{numpy}) and the
state-of-the-art \emph{MaskablePPO} over the masked resource set (sb3-contrib,
isolated venv, 600k steps). The reward was aligned to the objective but deliberately
\emph{not} tuned to the evaluation score. The postpone action was found to destabilize
training under a fairness penalty (it became a way to avoid assigning), so it is
strongly penalized. Like every method, the policy plugs into the swappable
\emph{AllocationStrategy.pick(candidates, context)} interface (a postpone returns
\emph{None}). The REINFORCE policy serializes to JSON, so its deployment needs no
machine-learning dependency at runtime. The training hyperparameters and learning
curve are in the Appendix (Table~\ref{tab:rl_hparams}, Fig.~\ref{fig:rl_curve}). The
policy is evaluated against the other methods in Sec.~\ref{ssub:rl-eval}.
