ParkSong-Composite completed all 380/380 fixed-route runs across seeds 1–5. Its mean cycle time was
32.2304 h, mean waiting time was 73.3656 min, Gini was 0.5284, and weighted fairness was 0.0044.

The method created 1,580 reservations, of which 474 were used, corresponding to a 30.0\% utilization rate.
The prediction-aware mechanism was therefore operational, but most reservations did not result in assignments.

The results confirm that predictive reservations were successfully integrated into the simulator. However, the
comparatively high cycle and waiting times indicate that strategic idling was costly under imperfect predictions
and limited processing-time coverage.

ParkSong-Composite should therefore be interpreted as a functioning anticipatory allocation mechanism, not as
evidence that predictive reservations outperform reactive heuristics in every setting.
