\subsubsection{Reinforcement-Learning Policy}\label{ssub:rl-eval}
\textit{(Aldo Patrone)}\quad
\textbf{Setup.} The paper-faithful policy (Sec.~\ref{subsec:rl}) is evaluated in the discrete-event
environment it is trained in, the setting Middelhuis et al.\ also evaluate in, against four
baselines that make the same $(\text{resource},\text{activity})$ decisions: random, round-robin,
shortest-queue and most-experienced. All are scored on the paper's objective (mean cycle time) and
on load fairness over $25$ evaluation episodes (Table~\ref{tab:alloc_sim}). The learning curve is in
Fig.~\ref{fig:rl_curve} and the auxiliary numpy baselines in Fig.~\ref{fig:alloc_cmp_plot}.

\textbf{Result.} The finding is a robust \emph{negative} one: even with the full paper MDP, a
queue-aware $2|R|+|A|$ state, the $(r,a)$-pair action space with masking and the cycle-time reward,
the learned policy does not beat the simple heuristics. Cycle time is nearly flat across all methods
($4.5$--$4.9$\,h), so the allocation choice barely moves it. On fairness, shortest-queue is clearly
best ($0.158$) while the learned policy ($0.424$) does not improve on it. Most-experienced attains
the lowest cycle time ($4.46$\,h) by concentrating work on skilled resources, at the cost of
fairness. The learning curve confirms the agent trains (returns rise and plateau over $750$
episodes), so this is not an artifact of a broken run.

\textbf{Interpretation.} The explanation is structural and matches the paper's own observation that
well-matched heuristics are strong: shortest-queue is near-optimal for a load-balancing fairness
objective, and in this process the cycle time is dominated by arrivals and processing times rather
than by which eligible resource is chosen. Figure~\ref{fig:rl_load} makes this visible,
shortest-queue spreads work most evenly while the learned policy concentrates it on few resources.
The applicability answer the assignment asks for is therefore that, for this process and objective, a
learned allocation policy is \emph{not} justified over a one-line heuristic. Deep RL would pay off in
settings with sharper resource-activity specialization or binding capacity constraints, where the
assignment actually changes the cycle time.

\textbf{Deployment.} The trained policy also deploys onto the integrated \emph{SimulationEngineCore}
through the \emph{PPOAllocation} strategy (the per-task reduction of Sec.~\ref{subsec:rl}), which we
validated structurally. A full integrated multi-seed rerun of the deep model requires the engine's
XES reader and the original processing-time model together with the sb3 stack in a single
environment. The integrated baseline comparison across all group methods is reported in
Sec.~\ref{ssub:cross-eval}.
