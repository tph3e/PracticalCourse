\subsubsection{Simple Metrics}\label{ssub:metrics}
\textit{(Aldo Patrone)}\quad
Three metrics quantify allocation quality, all computed from the simulation event log
(identical schema to the real log, so they are validated against it).
\emph{Average cycle time} is the per-case span. \emph{Average resource occupation}
is busy time over available time, where busy time is the \emph{union} of a
resource's activity intervals rather than their sum, since real resources juggle
overlapping waiting work items and summing would exceed $100\%$. The denominator is
each resource's available time from the learned 1.6 calendars rather than the
wall-clock window, which is far more meaningful (Table~\ref{tab:metrics_ref}: $26\%$
vs.\ $93\%$). \emph{Resource fairness} is the Gini coefficient over per-resource load
(lower is fairer), with a weighted variant that normalizes by availability. The same
\emph{compute\_all} routine doubles as the reward signal for the RL policy
(Sec.~\ref{subsec:rl}) and as the metric basis for the overall method comparison
(Sec.~\ref{ssub:cross-eval}).
