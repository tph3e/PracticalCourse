\subsubsection{Simple Metrics}\label{ssub:metrics}
\textit{(Aldo Patrone)}\quad
Three metrics quantify allocation quality, all computed from the simulation event log
(identical schema to the real log, so they are validated against it).
\emph{Average cycle time} is the per-case span. \emph{Average resource occupation}
is busy time over available time, where busy time is the \emph{union} of a
resource's activity intervals rather than their sum, since real resources juggle
overlapping waiting work items and summing would exceed $100\%$. A resumed activity is
timed from its resume event, so the suspend and resume transitions the simulator emits
enter the busy interval correctly. The denominator is each resource's available time
from the learned availability calendars rather than the wall-clock window, which is far
more meaningful (Table~\ref{tab:metrics_ref}: $27\%$ vs.\ $96\%$). \emph{Resource fairness} is the Gini coefficient over per-resource load
(lower is fairer), with a weighted variant that normalizes by availability. The same
\emph{compute\_all} routine doubles as the reward signal for the RL policy
(Sec.~\ref{subsec:rl}) and as the metric basis for the overall method comparison
(Sec.~\ref{ssub:cross-eval}).

Formally, for cases $C$, resources $R$ and a resource's activity intervals
$[s_{r,k}, e_{r,k}]$, the three metrics of Lecture~6 are
\[
\overline{CT}=\frac{1}{|C|}\sum_{c\in C}\Big(\max_{e\in c}t(e)-\min_{e\in c}t(e)\Big),
\]
\[
\quad
\overline{occ}=\frac{1}{|R|}\sum_{r\in R}\frac{\big|\bigcup_k[s_{r,k},e_{r,k}]\big|}{\mathrm{avail}_r},
\]
\[
G=\frac{\sum_{i}\sum_{j}\lvert x_i-x_j\rvert}{2\,|R|\sum_i x_i}\quad(\text{lower is fairer}),
\]
where $\overline{CT}$ is the per-case span (Def.~14 of~\cite{middelhuis2025rl}), the busy
time is the \emph{union} of intervals, $\mathrm{avail}_r$ is the available time from the learned
calendars (Sec.~\ref{subsec:resources}), and the fairness Gini $G$ is taken over the per-resource
load $x_r$ (busy seconds, or availability-normalized $x_r=\text{busy}_r/\mathrm{avail}_r$ for the
weighted variant), a scale-invariant form of the course's ``deviation from the average
occupation''.
