\subsubsection{Simple Metrics}\label{ssub:metrics}
\textit{(Aldo Patrone)}\quad
Three metrics quantify allocation quality, all computed from the simulation event log
(identical schema to the real log, so they are validated against it). For cases $C$,
resources $R$ and a resource's activity intervals $[s_{r,k},e_{r,k}]$:

\emph{Average cycle time} is the mean per-case span from first to last event
(Def.~14 of~\cite{middelhuis2025rl}),
\[
\overline{CT}=\frac{1}{|C|}\sum_{c\in C}\Big(\max_{e\in c}t(e)-\min_{e\in c}t(e)\Big).
\]

\emph{Average resource occupation} is busy time over available time,
\[
\overline{occ}=\frac{1}{|R|}\sum_{r\in R}\frac{\big|\bigcup_k[s_{r,k},e_{r,k}]\big|}{\mathrm{avail}_r}.
\]
Busy time is the \emph{union} of a resource's activity intervals rather than their sum, since
overlapping waiting work items would otherwise exceed $100\%$, and a resumed activity is timed
from its resume event. The denominator is each resource's available time from the learned
calendars (Sec.~\ref{subsec:resources}) rather than the wall-clock window, which is far more
meaningful (Table~\ref{tab:metrics_ref}: $27\%$ vs.\ $96\%$).

\emph{Resource fairness} is the Gini coefficient over the per-resource load $x_r$ (lower is fairer),
\[
G=\frac{\sum_{i}\sum_{j}\lvert x_i-x_j\rvert}{2\,|R|\sum_i x_i}.
\]
The load $x_r$ is busy seconds, or the availability-normalized $x_r=\text{busy}_r/\mathrm{avail}_r$
for the weighted variant, a scale-invariant form of the course's ``deviation from the average
occupation''.

The same \emph{compute\_all} routine doubles as the reward signal for the RL policy
(Sec.~\ref{subsec:rl}) and as the metric basis for the overall method comparison
(Sec.~\ref{ssub:cross-eval}).
